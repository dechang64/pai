# Behavioral Economics of Charitable Giving

## Warm-Glow Giving Theory (Andreoni 1990)
James Andreoni's warm-glow model explains why people give to charity even when their contribution has negligible impact on the public good. Donors derive utility from the act of giving itself — the "warm glow." Key implications:
1. Donors respond to personal connection and emotional appeals
2. Matching grants increase giving (donors feel their impact is doubled)
3. Recognition and social proof amplify warm-glow effects
4. Tax deductions provide a "price discount" that increases giving quantity

## Intention-Action Gap in Charitable Giving
Research shows 70% of Americans express intent to give to charity, but only 33% actually do. Key barriers:
1. **Decision Fatigue**: Too many charity choices overwhelm donors
2. **Friction**: Complex donation processes reduce completion rates
3. **Timing**: Donors intend to give "later" but forget
4. **Trust**: Uncertainty about charity effectiveness reduces giving

## LLM-Assisted Giving (White et al. 2026)
A pre-registered experiment (N=1,949) demonstrated that LLM-powered giving advisors increased donation amounts by 45.9% compared to control. Key findings:
1. Personalized recommendations were more effective than generic advice
2. Providing cost-effectiveness information increased average gift size
3. Tax optimization suggestions increased likelihood of giving from appreciated assets
4. The effect was strongest for first-time donors and those giving <$1,000

## Nudges for Charitable Giving
Evidence-based nudges that increase charitable giving:
1. **Default Options**: Pre-selected donation amounts increase giving by 30-40%
2. **Social Proof**: "9 out of 10 donors give $50" increases average gift by 15%
3. **Anchoring**: Showing a high suggested amount first increases average gift
4. **Reciprocity**: Small gifts to donors increase subsequent giving by 25%
5. **Identity**: Framing giving as "who you are" rather than "what you do" increases commitment

## Matching Gift Effectiveness
Matching grants are one of the most powerful tools for increasing charitable giving:
- 1:1 match increases giving by 20-30%
- 2:1 match increases giving by 30-50%
- 3:1 match shows diminishing returns (only 10-15% additional increase)
- The match rate matters less than the existence of a match
