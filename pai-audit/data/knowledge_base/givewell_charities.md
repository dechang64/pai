# GiveWell Top Charities — Cost-Effectiveness Summary

## GiveDirectly
GiveDirectly sends cash directly to people living in extreme poverty. Recipients receive approximately $1,000 via mobile money transfer, with no strings attached. Independent RCTs show cash transfers increase consumption, assets, and psychological well-being. Cost per life significantly improved: approximately $5,000-$7,500 per life equivalent saved when accounting for long-term income effects. GiveDirectly has delivered over $600 million in transfers since 2009, serving more than 1.5 million recipients across Kenya, Uganda, Rwanda, Liberia, Malawi, DRC, Mozambique, Togo, and the US.

## Against Malaria Foundation (AMF)
AMF funds long-lasting insecticide-treated nets (LLINs) distributed by in-country partners. Each net costs approximately $5 and protects two people for 3-4 years. AMF's cost per life saved is estimated at $3,000-$5,000, making it one of the most cost-effective interventions in global health. AMF has distributed over 200 million nets across Africa, protecting over 400 million people. Post-distribution monitoring shows 85%+ net usage rates.

## Malaria Consortium
Malaria Consortium implements seasonal malaria chemoprevention (SMC) for children under 5 in the Sahel region. SMC involves monthly doses of antimalarial drugs during the high-transmission season. Cost per child fully protected for one season is approximately $5. SMC reduces malaria incidence by 75% in target areas. Malaria Consortium has reached over 50 million children across 13 countries since 2013.

## Helen Keller International (HKI)
HKI implements vitamin A supplementation (VAS) programs for children under 5. Each dose costs approximately $1. VAS reduces all-cause child mortality by 12-24%. HKI has delivered over 1 billion vitamin A supplements since 1990. The program operates in 14 countries across Africa and Asia, reaching approximately 100 million children annually.

## New Incentives
New Incentives uses conditional cash transfers to increase vaccination rates in Nigeria. Caregivers receive small cash incentives when their children complete routine immunizations. Cost per fully vaccinated child is approximately $7.50. The program has increased immunization coverage by 20-30 percentage points in target areas. Over 200,000 children have been vaccinated through the program since 2018.

## Evidence on Cost-Effectiveness
GiveWell's analysis uses DALYs (Disability-Adjusted Life Years) averted as the primary metric. One DALY represents one year of healthy life lost. The most cost-effective interventions achieve $50-$200 per DALY averted. For comparison, the WHO considers interventions costing less than $100 per DALY as "highly cost-effective." GiveWell's top charities consistently achieve $50-$150 per DALY averted, making them 10-100x more cost-effective than typical charitable donations.
